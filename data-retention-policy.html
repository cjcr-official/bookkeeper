Data Retention and Disposal Policy

Case Johnston Computer Repair, LLC — “Bookkeeper” application
Effective July 10, 2026 · Reviewed annually

1. Purpose and scope

This policy defines how Case Johnston Computer Repair, LLC (“we,” “us”) retains and disposes of the data handled by the Bookkeeper application. It covers all customer/consumer data stored by the application, including any financial account information obtained through the Plaid API. We are a sole-proprietor business with no employees; the owner is responsible for enforcing this policy and reviewing it at least annually.

2. Data we retain and retention periods

Data categoryWhere storedRetention period
Account & business profile (login email, company details) Supabase (encrypted at rest) For the life of the account; deleted on account closure or request.
Financial records entered by the user (invoices, customers, expenses, accounts, mileage, jobs, payments) Supabase (encrypted at rest) Retained while the account is active so records remain available for the user's own tax/bookkeeping needs; deleted on account closure or request.
Uploaded receipt / statement files Supabase Storage (private buckets) Until the user deletes the item or the account; deleted on account closure or request.
Bank account access tokens (from Plaid) Server-side only (Worker service key; never exposed to the browser) Held only while the bank is linked; deleted immediately on disconnect or account deletion.
Bank transaction data (from the Plaid API) Not stored Retrieved live for on-screen reconciliation and not retained after the session.

We retain data only for as long as it is needed to provide the service to the user, or as required to comply with applicable law. We do not sell data and do not retain it for secondary purposes such as advertising.

3. Disposal methods

When data reaches the end of its retention period, or when a user deletes it, we dispose of it as follows:

  • Database records are permanently deleted from Supabase (a managed, encrypted-at-rest Postgres service).
  • Stored files (receipts/statements) are deleted from the private Storage buckets.
  • Bank access tokens are revoked at Plaid (item removal) and the stored token record is deleted.
  • The login/identity is removed from the authentication system.
  • Provider backups containing residual copies are purged automatically on our infrastructure providers' standard backup-rotation cycles.

4. Deletion triggers

  • User self-service: a user can permanently delete individual records at any time, disconnect a linked bank at any time, or delete their entire account and all associated data from within the application (Settings › Account › Delete account), confirmed by typing “DELETE.”
  • User request: a user may email us to have their account and data deleted, and we will action it.
  • Account closure / inactivity: data associated with a closed account is deleted.

5. Data handled through the Plaid API

Consumer bank data received from Plaid is minimized: transaction data is pulled live for reconciliation and is not stored, and the only Plaid-derived item retained is the access token, which is held server-side only and destroyed as soon as the bank is disconnected or the account is deleted.

6. Security of retained data

All retained data is encrypted at rest, isolated per user with row-level security, and transmitted only over TLS 1.2 or higher. Access requires authentication, with multi-factor / passkey protection available on the account.

7. Review

This policy is reviewed at least once per year, and whenever there is a material change to how the application stores or processes data, and updated as needed.
